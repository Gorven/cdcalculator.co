Frequently Asked Questions

What is a CD (Certificate of Deposit)?

A CD is a time deposit that typically offers a higher interest rate than a regular savings account in exchange for leaving your money untouched for a fixed period.

How is CD interest calculated?

CD interest is typically calculated using compound interest, which means you earn interest on your interest as well as your principal.

What happens if I withdraw early?

Early withdrawal usually results in penalties that can reduce your earnings. The specific penalty depends on your bank and CD terms.
